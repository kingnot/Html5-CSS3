Telephone

A telephone, or phone, is a telecommunications device that permits two or more users to conduct a conversation when they are not in the same vicinity of each other to be heard directly. That is basically a process from voice to electronic signals and back to voice. You might feel this is nothing surprising, but it was really a magnificent development at that time. Who invented telephone, which was frequently disrupted, is not really important. As this invention was eventually work of many people, let's just remember some of them: Charles Bourseul, the French engineer, who wrote the first design of a telephone in a public memorandum in 1854; Alexander Graham Bell, who was the first to be awarded a patent for the electric telephone by USPTO in March 1876, and many others.

Thanks to the great work of these people, the communication between people became much easier, and the world became much smaller. Almost every person on the earth is benefiting from this great invention, no matter which kind of phone they are using, it is indispensable!

Did You Know?

- On March 10, 1876 — The first successful telephone transmission of clear speech using a liquid transmitter when Bell spoke into his device, Mr. Watson, come here, I want to see you. and Watson heard each word distinctly.

- On April 27, 1877 — Edison files for a patent on a carbon (graphite) transmitter. The patent 474,230 was granted May 3, 1892, after a 15 year delay because of litigation. Edison was granted patent 222,390 for a carbon granules transmitter in 1879.

- By the end of 2009, there were a total of nearly 6 billion mobile and fixed-line telephone subscribers worldwide. This included 1.26 billion fixed-line subscribers and 4.6 billion mobile subscribers.
